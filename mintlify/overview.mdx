Lemon Search is the engine that turns a half-typed query into the right local
business, ranked well, in under the blink of an eye. It searches roughly 23,000
real Miami businesses (restaurants, salons, gyms, tow trucks, wedding
photographers, and everything in between) and returns the top 15 results as you
type. The user interface is intentionally thin: the engine is the product.

## The problem it solves

People do not type clean queries. They misspell, they abbreviate, they describe a
vibe instead of a name, and they expect the right answer instantly. A plain
keyword search fails all of that: it cannot tell that "joes barbr shop" means
"Joe's Barber Shop", that "cheap restaurants" is about price, or that a sushi
restaurant two blocks away should beat one across the city. Lemon Search is built
to feel like it understands what you meant, not just what you typed.

## What makes it good

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Real typo tolerance" icon="spell-check">
    "steaak" finds steak, "joes barbr" finds Joe's Barber Shop. Misspellings,
    dropped letters, and accents (café vs cafe) all resolve to the right
    business.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Smart intent" icon="brain">
    "cheap restaurants" prioritizes affordable spots by price tier. "chill place
    to work" finds the right vibe even with no keyword to match on, using a small
    local language model.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sub-100ms speed" icon="bolt">
    Every keystroke returns in well under a tenth of a second at the 95th
    percentile. Speed is treated as a hard constraint, measured under load, not
    an afterthought.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Ranking that feels right" icon="trophy">
    Results are scored on seven signals (distance, rating, popularity, friends,
    claimed status, photos, open-now) weighted differently for a restaurant than
    for a tow truck.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
